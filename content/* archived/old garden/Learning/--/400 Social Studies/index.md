# Course Description 
AP Human Geography is a **challenging course designed to replicate or exceed the experience of a semester long introductory college human geography course**. The course focuses on the **connection, processes, and effects of human populations on the planet.** The AP curriculum **focuses on seven units of study**, which are as shown below:

- Unit 1 — Thinking Geographically
	- [[1.1 - Intro to Maps and Scale]]
	- [[1.3 - Regions and FRQs]]
	- [[1.4 - Time Space Compression]]
	- [[1.5 - Scales of Analysis]]
- Unit 2 — Population and [[Migration]] 
	- [[2.3 - The Demographic Transition Model]]
	- [[2.4 - Article Analysis Skills]]
	- [[2.6 - Migration Patterns and Processes]]
- Unit 3 — Cultural Patterns and Processes
	- [[3.1 - Creating Culture]]
	- [[3.9 - Geography of the Abrahamic Religions IP]]
	- [[3.10 - Geography of the Dharmic Religions NS]]
	- [[Unit 3 - Study Guide]]
- Unit 4 — Political Patterns and Processes 
	- [[4.2 - Wallerstein and The Berlin Conference]]
	- [[Unit 4 - Study Guide]]
- Unit 5 — Agricultural and Rural Land Use 
	- [[5.1 - Intro to Agriculture]]
	- [[5.2 - Settlement Patterns & Survey Method]]
	- [[Unit 5 - Study Guide]]
- Unit 6 — Cities and [[Urban]] Land Use
	- [[6.1 - Urban Simulation]]
