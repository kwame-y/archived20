## The Rise of [[World City|World Cities]]
>**Cities have been central to political and economic power globally**, with Bergen, Norway, serving as a prime example during the fourteenth and sixteenth centuries due to its trade significance.
- Over centuries, the significance of cities like Bergen shifted as other cities (e.g., [[London]], [[Amsterdam]], [[Paris]], [[Rome]], [[Berlin]], [[Chicago]],[[ New York City]], and [[St. Petersburg]])[^1] **rose to prominence, especially with [[Industrialisation]].**
- Today, [[World City|world cities]] are pivotal as command and control centers of the global economy, *housing multinational corporations, financial services, media companies, and economic/business services.* 
	- They are characterized by **cultural diversity, innovation, and environmental focus.** ==**In the beginning, only [[New York]], [[London]], and [[Tokyo]] were considered world [[cities]],** but now *[[Paris]], [[Hong Kong]], [[Singapore]], [[Los Angeles]], [[Sydney]], and [[Berlin]] are also included.*==
## Economic Power and [[World Cities]]
- ==[[World City|World cities]] attract a global cosmopolitan population,== indicating a spatial concentration of economic power. **[[London]] is noted for its high concentration of high-net-worth individuals.**
- Reflecting the tastes and security concerns of the wealthy, **[[gated communities]]** within [[World City|world cities]] offer ==exclusive services and amenities, contributing to social segregation.==
## Connections and [[World Cities]]
- **[[World City|World cities]] facilitate [[globalisation]] through networks in transportation, communication, and business services.**
- ==[[World City|World cities]] feature efficient transportation hubs, including harbors, airports, and rail systems, facilitating global and domestic connectivity.==
- Advanced communications systems in [[World City|world cities]] ensure immediate access to global information for decision-makers and businesses.
- Financial markets, international law, and marketing firms in [[World City|world cities]] provide essential services to multinational corporations and play a role in global economic dynamics.

>[!tip]- Key Points 
>- [[World City|World cities]] have evolved from historical trade centers to modern hubs of global economic, political, and cultural influence.
>- They are characterized by their ability to attract a diverse, talented population, their role in global decision-making, and their infrastructure supporting global connectivity.
>- The rise of [[World City|world cities]] reflects broader trends in globalisation, including the movement of the global cosmopolitan class and the development of [[gated communities]] as a response to income inequality and security concerns.
