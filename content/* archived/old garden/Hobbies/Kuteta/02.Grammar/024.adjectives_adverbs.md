# Modifier Positioning

## Adjective Placement
- Adjectives appear **before the noun** they describe.
- Structure: **[adjective] + [noun]**

> _pika nako_ → "bright fire"

## Adverb Placement
- Adverbs appear **before the verb** they modify.
- Structure: **[adverb] + [verb]**

> _kuwi dusel_ → "quietly sing"

---

# Comparison Structure

Kuteta uses standalone modifier roots with helper words to express comparisons.

- **Positive / Negative Degree**
> _pika selo_ → "big tree"

- **Comparative** ("more")
	- Use **maka** ("more") before the modifier:
> _maka pika selo_ → "bigger tree"

- **Superlative** ("most")
	- Use **maka maka** ("most") before the modifier for emphasis:
> _maka maka bara oremi_ → "The strongest person."

- Equative ("as...as")
- Use **kana...kana** around the modifier:
> _kana pika kana selo_ → "as big as the tree"

# Core Modifier Roots

| English | Kuteta Root | IPA     | Notes                             |
| ------- | ----------- | ------- | --------------------------------- |
| big     | pika        | [pi.ka] | Common descriptive adjective      |
| small   | milo        | [mi.lo] | From root _mi_                    |
| bright  | taya        | [ta.ja] | Related to sun _toya_             |
| dark    | ŋura        | [ŋu.ɾa] | From root _ŋu_                    |
| fast    | seku        | [se.ku] | Related to verb root _se_ (to be) |
| slow    | nolu        | [no.lu] | Soft, dragged phonetic feel       |
| strong  | bara        | [ba.ɾa] | Bold sound, heavy                 |
| weak    | sali        | [sa.li] | Light and soft                    |
| quietly | kuwi        | [ku.wi] | Diminutive of _kulo_ (to speak)   |
| loudly  | tulo        | [tu.lo] | From root _tu_ (emphatic voice)   |

# Examples
1. **oremi li kuwi dusel-ta**  
    “The person sang quietly.”
2. **tulo kulo-ta-za**  
    “spoke loudly (rudely).”
3. **kana seku kana nemi li kulo-no**  
    “The cat will speak as fast as”
