# Particles

| Function     | Particle | Position                   | Example                       | Notes                                                                                               |
| ------------ | -------- | -------------------------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Topic**    | **li**   | After topic noun           | _remi **li** kulo-ta_         | Marks the topic. Always appears after the topic noun.                                               |
| **Subject**  | **ka**   | After subject noun         | _remi li remi-**ka** kulo-ta_ | Emphasizes or clarifies the subject (the thing carrying out the verb). Optional if subject = topic. |
| **Object**   | **na**   | After object noun          | _nemi **na** miya-no_         | Marks the direct object of the verb.                                                                |
| **Copula**   | **tu**   | Between subject & noun/adj | _remi li tu bara_             | Optional "to be" marker. Use for clarity or formal tone.                                            |
| **Negation** | **ne**   | Before verb                | _remi li ne kulo-no_          | Negates the verb. May also appear as a separate particle.                                           |
| **Question** | **ke**   | Sentence-final             | _kute na kulo-no ke?_         | Turns a statement into a question. Always goes at the end.                                          |

# Example Sentences

**Basic statement:**

> _oremi li neko na miya-ta._  
> "The person saw the cat."

**With emphasis on subject:**

> _oremi li remi ka nemi na miya ta._  
> "As for the person, it was Remi who saw the cat."

**With copula:**

> _neko li tu bara._  
> "The cat is strong."

**Negative sentence:**

> _oremi li ne kulo-no._  
> "The person will not speak."

**Yes-no question:**

> _oremi li kulo-no ke?_  
> "Will the person speak?"
